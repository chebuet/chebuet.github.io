Department History

A journey of six decades — from founding to forefront

Heritage

Our Story

The Department of Chemical Engineering at BUET has a rich history spanning more than six decades. What began as a modest academic unit has grown into one of the most respected chemical engineering departments in South Asia, characterized by its commitment to quality education, rigorous research, and service to society.

1961

Department Established

The Department of Chemical Engineering was founded as part of the erstwhile East Pakistan University of Engineering and Technology (EPUET), which later became BUET. The department launched its first batch of B.Sc. Chemical Engineering students with a pioneering faculty.

1971

Independence and Resilience

Through the turbulent period of Bangladesh's independence, the department continued its educational mission. EPUET was renamed BUET, and the department emerged stronger, recommitting itself to serving the new nation's industrial and engineering needs.

1980s

Graduate Programs Introduced

The department launched its M.Sc. (Engineering) program, enabling postgraduate education and research. This decade saw a significant expansion of the faculty and the establishment of several specialized research laboratories.

1990s

PhD Program and Research Growth

A formal Ph.D. program was introduced, alongside increased collaboration with international universities and research institutions. The BRTC (BUET Research and Training Centre) began offering professional training programs in partnership with the department.

2000s

Modernisation and Expansion

The department underwent significant infrastructure expansion with new laboratory facilities, computing resources, and an updated curriculum aligned with global accreditation standards. International partnerships with universities in the USA, UK, Japan, and Australia were formalized.

2010s

Research Excellence and International Recognition

Faculty members published research in top-tier international journals. Several externally-funded research projects from the World Bank, UGC, and international agencies were hosted. The department's alumni network expanded to all continents, with graduates holding leadership positions in major multinational companies.

2020s

Digital Transformation and Sustainable Future

The department embraced digital learning technologies and computational tools. Research focus expanded to address climate change, renewable energy, and sustainable manufacturing. The department continues to innovate its curriculum to prepare graduates for the fourth industrial revolution and beyond.
