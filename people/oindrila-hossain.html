Biography

Dr. Oindrila Hossain is an Assistant Professor in the Department of Chemical Engineering at Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET). She completed her doctoral degree at North Carolina State University in 2024 under the supervision of Prof. Qingshan Wei, with a dissertation titled "Advances in Rapid, Low-Cost Miniaturized VOC Sensors for Detecting Plant Pathogens and Monitoring Vegetable Quality."

Her research sits at a compelling intersection of chemical sensing, agricultural technology, and public health diagnostics. During her PhD, she contributed to developing smartphone-based colorimetric sensing platforms, wearable plant sensors, and field-portable diagnostic tools that can detect plant diseases through volatile organic compound (VOC) analysis — without any physical contact with or damage to the plant. Her work on a leaf-attachable chemiresistive sensor array for real-time plant monitoring was published in the prestigious journal Matter (2021), and her smartphone-based VOC sensor for detecting ramorum blight was featured as an ACS Editors' Choice in ACS Sensors (2026).

At BUET, Dr. Hossain brings this cutting-edge sensing expertise to bear on challenges in disease diagnostics, agricultural food safety, and chemical safety — developing low-cost tools suitable for field deployment in resource-limited settings.
