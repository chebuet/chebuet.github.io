Research Interests

Solid Fuels & Gasification Coal / Biomass-to-Liquid Heterogeneous Catalysis Pyrolysis Hydrothermal Carbonization Syngas Conversion Life Cycle Assessment Techno-Economic Analysis Waste-to-Energy Environmental Pollution

Prof. Kabir's research programme centres on thermochemical conversion of solid fuels — coal, biomass, and waste streams — with a focus on producing clean energy carriers and valuable chemicals. His doctoral work at Monash University under Prof. Sankar Bhattacharya involved detailed process modelling and experimental investigation of Victorian brown coal gasification and synthesis of dimethyl ether (DME), using synchrotron-based characterisation techniques to probe catalyst structural changes in situ. Since returning to BUET, he has extended this expertise to biomass and municipal waste valorisation, studying hydrothermal carbonization of biogenic waste, lipid-extracted algae gasification, and syngas-to-liquid fuel conversion via heterogeneous catalysis. His work spans fundamental kinetics through to life-cycle and techno-economic assessment of energy conversion processes. Beyond research, Dr. Kabir is deeply invested in academic quality assurance, currently serving as Director of BUET's Institutional Quality Assurance Cell (IQAC) and as Member Secretary of the Board of Accreditation for Engineering and Technical Education (BAETE).
