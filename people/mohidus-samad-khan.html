Research Interests

Environmental Biotechnology Bioactive Papers & Paper Diagnostics Industrial Sustainability & Circularity Food Safety & Food Processing Water & Wastewater Treatment Biosurface Engineering Chemical & Occupational Safety Cleaner Production Biochemical Engineering Textile Waste Management

Dr. Khan is a sustainability and biotechnology expert whose research bridges environmental engineering and biomedical innovation. He is the founding director of the BET Research Group and the Industrial Sustainability Research (ISR) Group at BUET, and simultaneously serves as Director of the Institute of Appropriate Technology (IAT). His pioneering work on bioactive papers and paper diagnostics — including a blood-typing paper diagnostic that won the Australian Eureka Prize — has been published in leading journals such as Analytical Chemistry, Colloids and Surfaces B, and Current Opinion in Chemical Engineering. Beyond the lab, Dr. Khan has led multi-million-euro projects on textile sustainability with European funding, served as a WHO Technical Advisor on PPE quality during COVID-19, and contributed to national policy on water management and circular economy. To date, he has authored or co-authored over 85 technical articles including books, patents, and peer-reviewed papers.
