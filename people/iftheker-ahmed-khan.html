Research Interests

Environmental Engineering Nanotechnology Environmental Technology Technology Management Water & Wastewater Treatment Nanomaterials Synthesis 2D Materials (h-BN, Graphene) Microplastic Pollution Pollutant Fate & Transport

Dr. Khan's research group works at the intersection of environmental engineering and nanotechnology, developing advanced nanomaterials for water purification and investigating the fate and transport of emerging contaminants. His recent work includes the synthesis and application of hexagonal boron nitride (h-BN) nanostructures for dye removal, and the study of microplastic pollution in Bangladeshi waterways. His doctoral training at the University of South Carolina focused on environmental implications and applications of engineered nanomaterials.
