Research Interests

Fecal Sludge Management Wastewater Treatment Sanitation Engineering Hydrothermal Carbonization Biomass & Waste Valorization Cook Stove Improvement Environmental Engineering Solid Waste Management

Dr. Rahman's research focuses on environmental sanitation engineering, particularly the safe management and treatment of fecal sludge in low- and middle-income country contexts. He has conducted extensive field research on pit latrine management, fecal sludge characterization, and the assessment of treatment technologies in emergency humanitarian settings, including the Rohingya camps at Cox's Bazar, Bangladesh. His work spans hydrothermal carbonization of biogenic municipal waste and sewage sludge for energy and resource recovery, as well as CFD-based modelling of biomass cooking stoves to improve efficiency and reduce indoor air pollution. Dr. Rahman collaborates actively with international partners including icddr,b and international water and sanitation organisations.
