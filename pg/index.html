Academic Offerings
Postgraduate Programmes

The department offers research-oriented postgraduate programmes leading to the MSc, MEngg, and PhD degrees in Chemical Engineering and allied disciplines.

MSc

Master of Science in Chemical Engineering

A research-based programme combining advanced coursework with an independent thesis under the guidance of a faculty supervisor.

Credit Hours36 ThesisRequired (18 Credits) ModeFull-time / Part-time
MEngg

Master of Engineering (Chemical Engineering)

A project-based programme combining advanced coursework with an independent design/engineering project under faculty supervision.

Credit Hours36 ProjectRequired (6 Credits) ModeFull-time / Part-time
MSc

Master of Science in Biochemical Technology

An interdisciplinary thesis-based programme at the interface of biology, chemistry, and engineering for modern bioprocessing challenges.

Credit Hours36 ThesisRequired (18 Credits) ModeFull-time / Part-time
MSc

Master of Science in Energy Technology and Management

A thesis-based programme addressing energy systems, sustainability and policy — bridging technical and managerial perspectives.

Credit Hours36 ThesisRequired (18 Credits) ModeFull-time / Part-time
MSc

Master of Science in Process Safety Engineering

A thesis-based programme focused on hazard identification, risk assessment, and safety management in chemical process industries.

Credit Hours36 ThesisRequired (18 Credits) ModeFull-time / Part-time
PhD

Doctor of Philosophy in Chemical Engineering

A full research degree requiring original contribution to knowledge, culminating in a comprehensive dissertation and public defence.

Credit Hours54 Qualifying ExamAfter 2nd semester Proposal DefenceRequired
Joining the Programme
Admission Information

Applications are accepted once or twice a year for the April and October sessions. Admission is competitive and merit-based.

Eligibility — MSc / MEngg

  • BSc in Chemical Engineering or a closely related discipline from a recognised university
  • Written admission test covering Chemical Engineering fundamentals
  • Viva-voce examination by the departmental committee

Eligibility — PhD

  • BSc or MSc in Chemical Engineering or an equivalent recognised discipline
  • Statement of research intent and proposed topic
  • Prior identification of a willing faculty supervisor is strongly recommended

Research Mentorship
Supervisor Selection

All MSc and PhD students must identify and confirm a faculty supervisor within the first semester. Selection is subject to faculty availability and research alignment.

Supervisor Selection Portal

Submit your supervisor preference through the official online portal. New students should consult with the postgraduate coordinator before submitting.

Open Portal →

Supervisor Selection Guidelines

  • Review the research areas of all faculty members in the Faculty section before submitting
  • Contact your prospective supervisor in person or by email to discuss research compatibility prior to submission
  • Supervisor changes after allocation are exceptional and require written justification to the BPGS
